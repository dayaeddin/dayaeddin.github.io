  Biography
</h2>

<p>
  I am a <strong>Ph.D. graduate in Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing</strong> (February 2025) from
  the <strong>University of Abbas Laghrour, Algeria</strong>, with a research focus on <strong>Neural Machine Translation</strong>,
  <strong>Arabic NLP</strong>, and <strong>low-resource language processing</strong>. My doctoral dissertation,
  <em>"Conception of a Neural Model for Natural Language Translation"</em>, introduced hybrid attention mechanisms
  combining character-level CNN-BiLSTM architectures to enhance Arabic translation quality.
</p>

<p>
  My research spans <strong>multimodal learning</strong>, <strong>stochastic neural networks</strong>, and
  <strong>Arabic computational linguistics</strong>, with applications in sentiment analysis, part-of-speech tagging,
  and cross-lingual translation systems. I maintain active collaborations with international research institutions,
  including a research internship at the <strong>University of Malta</strong> (2023).
</p>

  Current Research Directions
</h2>

<p>
  My ongoing research investigates the intersection of <strong>stochastic differential equations</strong> and
  <strong>neural architectures</strong> for robust Arabic NLP in low-resource settings. Key projects include:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Multimodal Neural Machine Translation:</strong> Integrating visual information to enhance Arabic translation quality.</li>
  <li><strong>Character-Level Arabic Processing:</strong> Neural SDE-based models for POS tagging and sentiment classification.</li>
  <li><strong>Low-Resource Language Adaptation:</strong> Transfer learning and hybrid attention mechanisms for under-represented Arabic dialects.</li>
</ul>
